                    🌐 Atlasaurus

                    An interactive map of every country — borders, capitals, flags and facts — grouped into continents, with overseas territories drawn in. The sidebar has two modes at the top: Explore (browse the map freely) and Quiz (test yourself). Quiz has two sub-tabs: Challenge — scored rounds (the default) — and Practice — a self-paced map where everything hides as Country ?, City ? … and you click to reveal.

                    Explore is for browsing the world map freely — country names show from the start. The sidebar reads top to bottom: Map options first, then a list for each feature.

                    • Map options — switch a feature on to show it on the map with its real names: Countries, Cities, Mountains, Rivers, Lakes (capital cities show with a red ring among the Cities). Regions tints the map by grouping; Flags and Hover info overlay the map. Bigger cities and the major rivers, lakes and peaks come first, with more as you zoom in.
                    • Feature lists — switching a feature on enables its list below (Countries is always on). Every list filters by name, and clicking an entry zooms to it and opens its details. The country list adds Sort (name, population or area); Regions adds Group by — continents, UN regions, UN subregions (Caribbean, Western Europe…) or World Bank regions — and clicking a region highlights its countries and shows totals.
                    • Select a country (on the map or from a list) for a fact panel (population, area, GDP, currency, languages…) plus dashed lines to its territories (Alaska, Greenland, Réunion…). Click the ocean to clear.
                    • Map labels are just text — click any feature (country, city, peak, river or lake) for a detail box, with its Wikipedia link inside.
                    • Hover info shows a flag · name · capital tooltip at the cursor.

                    Want to test yourself instead? The Quiz tab's Practice sub-tab hides every name as Country ?, City ? … so you can guess and click to reveal.

                    The Quiz tab has two sub-tabs:

                    • Challenge — scored rounds (the default; below).
                    • Practice — a self-paced map where every feature hides as Country ?, City ?, Mountain ? … Guess from shape or position and click to reveal the real name (it stays revealed). Or switch a type on under Reveal to show all of its names at once. No score.

                    Challenge opens a Start screen where you set up one quiz: pick a category (Countries, Cities, Regions, Lakes, Mountains, Rivers), a type, and the number of questions (5 / 10 / 20), then Start quiz. Your best score for that exact setup is shown there (with a Reset). A correct answer is worth 5 pts, or 2 pts if you use the Show 5 options help. After the last question a summary shows your score and best, with Play again (same setup) or New quiz.

                    The types:

                    • By name — click the named country on the map. By flag / By capital — click the country on the map, or search and select it by name.
                    • Spot it — the reverse: a random country is highlighted and pinned on the map; type its name to identify it.
                    • Neighbour — find all countries that border the target: click them on the map, or search to add them, then Check. Found neighbours show green, missed ones and wrong picks are marked too.
                    • Cities — Name it: a city is marked on the map; search and pick its name. Which country: the city is named (not marked); click the country it's in or search and select it.
                    • Regions — a country is named; click any country in its continent on the map. The correct continent turns green, a wrong pick red.
                    • Mountains — Name it: a peak is marked; search and pick its name. Which country: the peak is named (not marked); click the country it's in or search and select it (border peaks accept any of their countries).
                    • Lakes / Rivers — Name it: the lake shape or river course is highlighted on the map; search and pick its name. Which country: the lake/river is named; click or search a country it touches — any one counts, and all are listed in the answer.
                    • Answers are revealed on the map (correct green, wrong red) with flag and name; click Next → for the next question, or Quit to return to Start.

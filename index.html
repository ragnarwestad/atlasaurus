                    🌐 Atlasaurus

                    An interactive map of every country — borders, capitals, flags and facts — grouped into continents, with overseas territories drawn in. The sidebar has two modes at the top: Explore (browse the map) and Quiz (test yourself).

                    Explore is for roaming the world map however you like. It opens in guess mode: every country, city, river, lake and mountain shows anonymously — Country ?, City ?, River ?, Lake ?, Mountain ?. Guess what each one is from its shape or position and click to check — the real name appears and stays revealed. When you'd rather just learn, switch a feature on in Map options to reveal all of its names at once.

                    The sidebar reads top to bottom: Map options first, then a list for each feature you switch on.

                    • Map options — each toggle reveals a whole feature's real names at once (a single click reveals just one). Countries, Cities, Mountains, Rivers and Lakes sit anonymously until then; Regions tints the map by grouping. Capitals, Flags and Hover info overlay the map. Bigger cities and the major named rivers, lakes and peaks come first, with more revealed as you zoom in — zoom in to read the labels.
                    • Feature lists — switching a feature on in Map options enables its list below (Countries is always on). Every list filters by name, and clicking an entry zooms to it and opens its details — countries, cities, peaks, rivers and lakes all behave the same way. The country list adds Sort (name, population or area); Regions adds Group by — continents, UN regions, UN subregions (Caribbean, Western Europe…) or World Bank regions — and clicking a region highlights its countries and shows totals.
                    • Select a country (on the map or from a list) for a fact panel (population, area, GDP, currency, languages…) plus dashed lines to its territories (Alaska, Greenland, Réunion…). Click the ocean to clear.
                    • Map labels are just text — click any feature (country, capital, city, peak, river or lake) for a detail box, with its Wikipedia link inside.
                    • Hover info shows a flag · name · capital tooltip at the cursor. Leave it off to quiz yourself — the hovered country still lights up, so you can guess its name from the shape, then click to check.

                    Switch to the Quiz tab (top of the sidebar) for several rounds with a running score. The panel reads top to bottom as collapsible sections — one per feature (Countries, Cities, Regions, Lakes, Mountains, Rivers), just like the Explore list. Open a section to start its quiz; opening another switches to it.

                    Inside Countries, pick a mode:

                    • By name — click the named country on the map. By flag / By capital — answer by Click on map or Select country (search by name).
                    • Spot it — the reverse: a random country is highlighted and pinned on the map; type its name to identify it.
                    • Neighbour — find all countries that border the target. Choose how to answer: Click on map or Select countries (search by name), then Check. Found neighbours show green, missed ones and wrong picks are marked too.
                    • Cities — Name it: a city is marked on the map; search and pick its name. Which country: the city is named (not marked); click the country it's in or search and select it.
                    • Regions — a country is named; click any country in its continent on the map. The correct continent turns green, a wrong pick red.
                    • Mountains — Name it: a peak is marked; search and pick its name. Which country: the peak is named (not marked); click the country it's in or search and select it (border peaks accept any of their countries).
                    • Lakes / Rivers — Name it: the lake shape or river course is highlighted on the map; search and pick its name. Which country: the lake/river is named; click or search a country it touches — any one counts, and all are listed in the answer.
                    • Wrong answers are revealed on the map (correct green, wrong red) with flag and name; use Skip or Next → to move on.
